About

The Swedish Schools Mini 2022 is a day-long, WSDC style tournament hosted by Debate Club of IEGS (IEGS) and Stockholm Debate Society (SDS) at Stockholm University. It will take place on the 26th of November this year and participation is completely free!

This is the second tournament hosted in a collaboration by SDS and IEGS in an initiative to spread debating in Sweden after the pandemic. The prior tournament, the Stockholm Highschool Debate Cup, was a huge success with teams from multiple highschools. This time, we are experimenting with allowing delegations from any institution, including universities. We hope that this will offer a more diverse and engaging experience for all participants.

The goal of the SSM is to be a smaller scale training tournament before the upcoming Swedish Schools Debating Championships, which has not been hosted for years, and will qualify participants to compete internationally at the World Schools Debating Championships (WSDC), on behalf of Sweden. There will also be more public workshops to come, all in the spirit of spreading the art of debate around the nation!

If you are a student or school teacher wishing to start/recieve help with running a debate society, please don't hesitate to reach out to us. On request, we may be able to send ambassadors to schools. Meanwhile, checkout our public resource folder for ways to improve debating, guides, past debate examples, and information about how to run debating societies.

Please keep scrolling to find out more information about rules, schedule, and the registration link! Happy debating :)

Why participate?

This was a promo video from the debate club of IEGS to join the club, but we think it also applies to this tournament :)

We highly encourage complete beginners to participate, as it is a very fun and educative experience. All our judges have had lots of experience both debating and judging, and the feedback one gains for every round immensely helps participants point out weaknesses, areas of improvement, and generally improve themselves as debators and critical thinkers.

It's a great opportunity to improve at english speaking, meet new friends, have thoughtful discussions, and improve confidence. One also gets an insider view to Stockholm University, and speak to highly experienced former debators. Lastly, there will even be free snacks! :)

(If you are in the IB program, it is also a great CAS opportunity!)
